Evaluation Criteria

There are two evaluation criteria: Good and Not Good.

When compared to native speakers' pronunciation, if 60% or more is correct, it is marked as Good. Otherwise, it is marked as Not Good.

Not Good does not mean your pronunciation is completely wrong, but it might confuse or lead to misinterpretation by foreigners. It's important to review the checkpoints of your pronunciation, and listen carefully to how your pronunciation sounds.

In addition, English has stress and accent, which are not present in Japanese, making it harder for foreigners to understand. But if the stress and accent are similar, it can make it easier for foreigners to comprehend.

Stress and accent will also be marked as Not Good if they need improvement.
