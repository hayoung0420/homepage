Voiced vs Voiceless Sounds: F vs P

The /f/ sound in English is voiceless, while the /p/ sound is also voiceless but produced with different mouth positioning!

  • Voiceless sounds like /f/ are produced without vibration of the vocal cords.
  • The /p/ sound is produced with a burst of air released after closing the lips.

For example, the word "fish" starts with a voiceless sound, while "pig" uses a voiceless sound produced with lip closure.

Tips for Practicing

Follow these steps to improve your pronunciation:

  • For /f/, place your top teeth lightly on your bottom lip.
  • For /p/, close your lips tightly and release a burst of air.
