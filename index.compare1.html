Voiced vs Voiceless Sounds: D vs TH

The /ð/ sound in English has two types: voiced and voiceless!

  • Voiced sounds like /ð/ require a vibration of the vocal cords.
  • Voiceless sounds like /θ/ do not have vibration of the vocal cords.

For example, the word "that" has a voiced sound, while "thing" uses a voiceless sound.

Tips for Practicing

Follow these steps to improve your pronunciation:

  • Put your tongue just a little bit between your teeth.
  • Pronounce the /ð/ sound by vibrating your vocal cords.
  • For /θ/, relax your vocal cords and do not vibrate.
